苍山徒步攻略：三条线路任你选

📅 2026

Cangshan's nineteen peaks — each hiking trail a different view. From the easy Jade Belt Road to the challenging Ximatang, there's one for you.

Why It's Worth the Trip

Cangshan's nineteen peaks stand in a screen-like row, snow on the summits all year, its gullies and cable cars intertwined. From the top you overlook Erhai like a mirror — the bone and soul of Dali. Plan a half day to a full day to wander slowly.

Experiences Not to Miss

Cangshan Mountain

Cangshan's nineteen peaks stand in a screen-like row, snow on the summits all year, its gullies and cable cars intertwined. From the top you overlook Erhai like a mirror — the bone and soul of Dali. Plan a half day to a full day to wander slowly.

Erhai Lake

Erhai is a crescent lake on the plateau, its water a clear blue with white gulls skimming low, lakeside villages scattered along the shore. Ride a stretch of the eco-corridor and you'll grasp the leisure of "wind, flowers, snow and moon."

Dali Old Town

Among the Dali old town's checkerboard lanes, under the Wuhua Tower hides the scent of tie-dye and roasted rushan cheese. At night, the bars of Foreigner Street and the old city wall each keep their own kind of time. Plan a half day to wander slowly.

🧭 Local Tips

  • The Ximatang cable car to the alpine lake is most worthwhile; plan a half day to a full day
  • It's high up — bring a windproof jacket
  • After rain, cloud bands wrap the peaks — best for photos
  • The west-shore eco-corridor is the prettiest ride; plan 1–2 days
  • Catch sunset from Caicun Pier at dusk
  • It's windy by the lake — dress warm on the bike

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to visit Dali?

March–May and September–November are best — Cangshan's azaleas and Erhai's breeze are just right. The June–August rainy season brings more showers but fewer crowds and quiet.

How many days suit Dali?

Three to four days is just right: a day circling Erhai, a day for Cangshan and the Three Pagodas, half a day each for Xizhou and Shuanglang, and the rest for idling in the old town.
